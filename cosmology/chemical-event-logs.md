- **Stage 1: Thermal Shock and Momentum Transfer (The Ejection Channel):** During the explosion, the outer layers of the star—rich in carbon—receive the primary momentum of the shockwave. Because these layers are less gravitationally bound, they are successfully expelled into the surrounding gas.
- **Stage 2: Gravitational Braking (The Fallback Channel):** In low-energy supernovae, the explosion energy is insufficient to overcome the binding energy of the inner layers. Oxygen-rich material, produced deep within the stellar interior, undergoes gravitational fallback into the central remnant.
- **Stage 3: Admissibility (The Filtered Yield):** The resulting chemical record is not a random sample; it is an irreversible admissibility record. Oxygen is “refused entry” into the system’s future gas phase, while carbon is admitted.

This process transforms the geometry of a stellar collapse into a permanent historical record, manifesting as an elevated C/O ratio at extremely low total metallicity.
